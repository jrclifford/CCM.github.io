About

A mediator with unmatched construction law depth

William D. Clifford, Esq. has been a Shareholder and Director at a leading regional law firm since 1984, where his practice centers on the drafting, negotiation, litigation, and mediation of construction and surety claims.

“The secret to resolution is persistence and creativity — and never giving up without using every available tool.”

Having represented owners, general contractors, subcontractors, suppliers, developers, public agencies, design professionals, and sureties throughout his career, Bill understands every seat at the table. He served as Adjunct Professor of Construction Law at Duquesne University School of Law from 2003 through 2019, and has mediated over 150 construction cases across a wide spectrum of project types and claim sizes.

Bill is proficient with remote mediation via Zoom and Microsoft Teams, and welcomes parties across Pennsylvania and beyond.

Process

How a mediation works

Bill's process is structured for efficiency and thoroughness — built to surface every issue and push toward a durable resolution.

01

Pre-mediation statements

Parties submit and exchange written statements at least one week before the session. Bill reviews all materials in advance.

02

Ex parte conferences

After reviewing statements, Bill holds confidential telephone conferences with counsel for each party to understand positions and objectives.

03

Mediation session

A brief joint plenary opens the day, followed by breakout sessions that guide parties through the facts and toward monetary positions and resolution.

04

Persistent resolution

Bill will not abandon a mediation without using every tool available — brackets, mediator proposals, and neutral evaluations — to reach settlement.

Services

Construction dispute resolution services

Bill has mediated disputes across every phase of construction and every party relationship — from ground-breaking to post-completion litigation.

Roughly half of Bill's 150+ mediated cases. Scope and change-order disputes, payment, and project-delivery conflicts between owners and their general contractors — on projects of every scale.

Scope-of-work disputes, payment and retainage, scheduling conflicts, and back-charge claims between general contractors and their subcontractors or suppliers — about fifty of his mediated matters.

Standard-of-care disputes, errors and omissions claims, and design-build issues between owners and their architects or engineers.

Liquidated damages, schedule impacts, acceleration claims, and inefficiency disputes arising from project delays and disruptions.

Surety bond claims, insurance coverage disputes, subrogation, and surety takeover issues involving sureties, owners, and contractors.

Unpaid contract balances, mechanic's lien disputes, False Claims Act issues, and ERISA claims — resolved to preserve cash flow and business relationships.
